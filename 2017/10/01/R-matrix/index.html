Fundamental of Quantitative Modeling

What is a model?

  • A formal description of a business process
  • It is almost always a simplification of a more complex structure. It is not that much simple, but should not be complex.
  • It typically relies upon a set of assumptions

Give an example of model:
The relationship beteen demard for, and price of, a product. If the demand increases, and the supply remains the same, there will be a shortage. This means the price will increase. This can help a company to figure out what price maximize profit.

However, model is used as a decision support tool, the result of any prediction or forecasting is not 100% correct.
